Research

Since I graduated from Vienna University, I have been involved in several EU and UK research projects, which are listed below in a time order (most recent to least recent).

Project I Explainable AI for UK agricultural land use decision-making, funded by UKRI

1. Improving Bayesian Spatial Modelling of Land Use Change with Features Extracted with a CNN
H Chen, PM Atkinson, C Nemeth, C Zhang and P Harrison
(under submission) 2022

We propose and explore a flexible framework to integrate features extracted with a deep convolutional neural network model for forecasting land use change, exploiting more fully the spatial information embodied in covariates.
2. Bayesian Hierarchical Spatial Modelling and Prediction of Land Use Change in the UK
H Chen, PM Atkinson, C Nemeth, C Zhang and P Harrison
(under submission) 2022

We adopt a Bayesian hierarchical spatial model to analyse UK land use change and its relationship with environmental and social drivers, considering fixed and random effects, with a case study in Oxfordshire.

Project II TUBERZONE, funded by UKRI and BBSRC

3. Investigations into the opportunity for spatial management of the quality and quantity of production in UK potato systems
JA Taylor, H Chen, M Smallwood and B Marshall
Field Crops Research (2018)

We conduct non-spatial and spatial statistical analysis of manual and sensor observations and discuss the potential opportunity for in-field site-specific potato management.

4. Conceptual spatial crop models for potato production
H Chen, I Leinonen, B Marshall and JA Taylor
Advances in Animal Biosciences: precision agriculture (ECPA), 2017

This study explores conceptual spatial models based on the potato crop models that simulate crop physical and physiological processes and predict yields and graded yields at a field-scale and the possibity to accomdate 'big data' for better decision making for precision farming.

Project III TEEB, funded by the EU

5. Wetland Quality as a Determinant of Economic Value of Ecosystem Services: an Exploration
H Chen, P Kumar and T Barker
arXiv 2022

This research introduces a wetland quality factor (a frenqently missed factor) into a meta-analysis and demonstrates that the influence of wetland quality on wetland economic values is statistically significant.

6. Estimating the welfare loss of climate change impact on corals
P Kumar and H Chen
Handbook on the Economics of Ecosystem Services and Biodiversity (2014)

Based on the prediction of frequency and severity of coral reef bleaching under climate change scenarios, we calculate the monetary losses of the coral reef ecosystem globally.

Project IV EUROLIMPACS, funded by the EU

7. Uncertainty analysis in a GIS-based multi-criteria analysis tool for river catchment management
H Chen, MD Wood, C Linstead and E Maltby
Environmental Modeling and Software (2011)

We developed an uncertainty analysis module for use within a GIS-based MCA tool for catchment management and its application to Tamar river catchment menonstrates its capability in decision-making in the context of climate change.

8. Methodology for down-scaling climate data provided by the PRUDENCE project for the HADAM3H/RCAO and ECHAM4/RCAO models
AJ Wade, RA Skeffington, U Nickus and H Chen
Euro-Limpacs Project Deliverable 433 (2008)

We provide the mathmatical formula for DownScaling climate data provided by the PRUDENCE project for the ADAM3H/ RCAO and ECHAM4/RCAO models.

Project V EVALUWET, funded by the EU

9. Development of a Wetland Evaluation Decision Support System (WEDSS) for Management Scenario Analysis
H Chen, C Linstead and E Maltby
under submission (2022)

A Wetland Evaluation Decision Support System (WEDSS) has been developed to assist wetland management decisions. This paper describes its structure and functionality, and its underlying mechanism for decision making support.

Other Projects:

10. Evidence of increase in woody vegetation in a river corridor, northwest England, 1984–2007
J Hooke and H Chen
Journal of Maps (2016)

We develop methods to map and measure changes in vegetation cover from aerial photographs and test them on a 5 km reach of the River Dane in Northwest England.

11. Cities evolution tree and applications to predicting urban growth
J Wang, X Liu, L Peng, H Chen, L Driskell and X Zheng
Population and Environment(2012)

We define and apply a new visualization and analysis tool—an evolution tree—showing how an understanding of evolutionary pathways taken by growing cities contributes to visualization and forecasting of urban development and spatial expansion.

12. Hand, foot and mouth disease: spatiotemporal transmission and climate
J Wang, Y Guo, G Christakos, W Yang, Y Liao, Z Li, X LI, S Lai and H Chen
International Journal of Health Geographics(2011)

We use a self-organizing map (SOM) algorithm to identify types of spatiotemporal HFMD transmission and their relations with climate.
